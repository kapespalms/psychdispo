Social Ref

Quick scripts for housing, 211, domestic violence, and Medicaid transport — for clinicians and social workers. Patient-facing blurbs live in the resource directory.

211 · statewide

Dial 211 — community resource navigation

211 connects callers to local food, housing, utilities, crisis, and benefits help. Available in most U.S. counties; hours vary by region.

Clinician script

"I'm going to give you a number you can call for help with food, housing, or bills — it's free and they know local programs. Dial 211 from any phone. If 211 doesn't work in your area, call the number on your patient sheet."

Patient blurb (≤ grade 5)

Dial 211 to find local help with food, housing, or crisis.
Housing · coordinated entry

Shelter & housing coordinated entry

Most communities use a single intake line for emergency shelter and housing navigation — not individual shelter walk-ins.

  • Ask: "Is there a bed tonight?" and "Do I need to call every day?"
  • Document the intake number on the patient sheet — avoid shelter addresses unless safe to print.
  • Pair with 211 when local coordinated entry is unknown.

Clinician script

"Shelter beds are usually found through one phone number for your area — not by going to a shelter door. Call the number on your sheet weekdays to ask about a bed tonight. If you can't get through, dial 211."

Patient blurb

Call weekdays to ask about a shelter bed tonight.
DV · safe print

Domestic violence hotlines

Local DV lines offer confidential safety planning and shelter referral. Default to discreet print on patient sheets unless the patient confirms safe to print names and addresses.

  • National DV hotline: 1-800-799-7233 (24/7)
  • Local lines may be 24/7 or business hours — verify in directory.
  • Never document partner name or address on patient-carried sheets when DV flag is active.

Clinician script

"If home isn't safe, you can call anytime — it's confidential and they won't tell anyone you called. The number is on your sheet. You don't have to leave today; they can help you plan."

Patient blurb

Call anytime if home is not safe — help is confidential.
NEMT · Medicaid

Medicaid non-emergency medical transport

Medicaid members often need rides to medical appointments through a broker — usually 2–3 business days advance notice.

  • Verify Medicaid eligibility before promising a ride.
  • Broker numbers vary by state — use verified directory entries.
  • Commercial insurance may have separate ride benefits.

Clinician script

"If you have Medicaid, you can call for a ride to your appointment. Call at least two or three days before your visit and say you need a medical ride."

Patient blurb

Call 2–3 days before your visit to ask about a medical ride.
