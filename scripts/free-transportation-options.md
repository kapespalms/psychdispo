# Free & No-Cost Transportation Options
## Research reference for PsychDispo discharge handouts

**Prepared:** June 2026  
**Audience:** Clinicians building psychiatric/social discharge resource packets; patient-facing blurbs for PsychDispo fields  
**Companion doc:** [`medicaid-medicare-transport-by-state.md`](medicaid-medicare-transport-by-state.md) — 51-state NEMT broker table  
**Scope:** National categories of free or no-cost transportation relevant to psychiatric discharge planning

---

## Executive summary (for clinicians)

**No single program guarantees a free ride.** The most reliable no-cost options for discharge planning are, in order of typical reliability:

1. **Medicaid NEMT** (when enrolled and eligible — not automatic; see companion doc)
2. **Medicare Advantage supplemental rides** (plan-specific; ~22–30% of individual MA plans in 2026)
3. **Veterans programs** (DAV vans, VTS, Beneficiary Travel reimbursement — VA-authorized care only)
4. **Dial 211 / Ride United** (local; fills gaps when insurance rides unavailable)
5. **ADA paratransit + reduced-fare public transit** (low cost, not always free; requires certification)

**Critical caveats for psychiatry discharge:**
- Most programs cover **medical destinations only** — not social visits, work, or grocery (unless a separate SSBCI/waiver benefit).
- **Advance notice** is almost always required (1–5 business days; paratransit often day-before).
- **Same-day rides are the exception**, not the rule.
- **Mobile crisis / 988 teams** may transport during an active crisis to stabilization — **not** for routine outpatient follow-up.
- **Reduced fare ≠ free** — patient still pays partial fare unless enrolled in a free-ride program (e.g., Illinois Benefit Access).

**Clinical takeaway:** Match the patient to their coverage first, then layer community backups (211, AAA volunteer drivers, paratransit). Always give a **specific phone number** and tell the patient to **call days ahead**.

## Quick decision tree (clinicians)

```
START: Patient needs ride to outpatient follow-up, pharmacy, therapy, or day program
│
├─ Has MEDICAID (or dual-eligible)?
│   ├─ YES → Call state NEMT broker (see medicaid-medicare-transport-by-state.md)
│   │         • GA, IN, IA, KY expansion adults: verify waiver limits first
│   │         • Dual-eligible: try Medicaid NEMT first, then check MA plan
│   │         • Advance notice: 2–5 business days typical
│   └─ NO  → Continue below
│
├─ Has MEDICARE only (no Medicaid)?
│   ├─ Medicare Advantage (Part C)? → Call member services on insurance card
│   │         • Ask: rides included? how many? medical only? advance notice?
│   │         • ~70% of individual MA plans do NOT include rides in 2026
│   └─ Original Medicare only? → No routine ride benefit
│         • Ambulance only if medically necessary (strict rules)
│         • Try: 211, Ride United, AAA volunteer driver, paratransit, clinic charity ride
│
├─ VETERAN with VA enrollment?
│   ├─ YES → Contact local VA facility / DAV Hospital Service Coordinator
│   │         • DAV Volunteer Transportation Network (free van, volunteer-staffed)
│   │         • VTS / VetRide portal (some facilities; 24-hr advance)
│   │         • Beneficiary Travel: reimbursement (not upfront free) if eligible
│   │         • Trips must be to VA or VA-authorized community care
│   └─ NO  → Continue below
│
├─ UNINSURED / underinsured?
│   ├─ Dial 211 (or text 898211 with zip in many areas)
│   │         • Ask for: medical ride programs, Ride United, senior transport, paratransit
│   ├─ Hospital/clinic charity ride (ask social work — often Uber/Lyft voucher via facility)
│   ├─ Nonprofit volunteer programs (ACS Road to Recovery if cancer; AAA if 60+)
│   ├─ Faith-based ride ministries (211 can refer)
│   └─ Public transit reduced fare (disability/Medicare/senior — partial cost)
│
├─ DISABILITY prevents using fixed-route bus?
│   ├─ Apply for ADA complementary paratransit (local transit agency)
│   │         • Eligibility process: 3–21 days; not same-day to start
│   │         • Once certified: book trips day-before (varies by agency)
│   └─ While waiting: 211, Medicaid NEMT if eligible, MA plan if included
│
├─ ACTIVE PSYCHIATRIC CRISIS (suicidal, psychotic, cannot safety plan)?
│   ├─ 988 (call/text/chat) → may dispatch mobile crisis team
│   │         • Transport to crisis stabilization/hospital IF team determines needed
│   │         • NOT for scheduling routine follow-up appointments
│   └─ 911 if immediate danger to self or others
│
└─ DOMESTIC VIOLENCE / shelter resident?
      • Many shelters provide or arrange transport to medical/legal appointments
      • Do NOT put shelter address on public handouts — refer through shelter case manager
      • National DV Hotline: 1-800-799-7233 (referral only; not a ride line)
```

### 1. Medicaid NEMT (Non-Emergency Medical Transportation)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Active Medicaid beneficiaries who lack other transportation (no working car, cannot drive, no usable transit, or medical need requires specialized vehicle). EPSDT youth (generally under 21) have stronger federal protections. **Exceptions:** GA Pathways, IN HIP, IA Wellness Plan, KY TEAMKY expansion adults, and some UT parent/caretaker groups may **not** have NEMT — see companion doc. |
| **Trips covered** | Medicaid-covered medical services: doctor/therapy/psychiatry, hospital outpatient, dialysis, pharmacy (varies by state), methadone/OTP (expanded in some states). **Not** social visits, work, grocery, or non-covered providers. |
| **How to request** | Call broker on Medicaid ID card or state line. Provide: member ID, appointment date/time/address, pickup address, mobility needs. **Schedule 2–5 business days ahead** (urgent/same-day only with provider verification in some states). |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Not automatic; public-transit-first rules common; no-shows may affect future rides; rural provider shortages; broker may assign bus pass instead of car; mileage reimbursement if household has a car (some states). |
| **patientBlurb** | `If you have Medicaid, call the ride number on your card 2–3 days before your visit.` |
| **transportPhone** | State broker from [`medicaid-medicare-transport-by-state.md`](medicaid-medicare-transport-by-state.md); format: `1-800-XXX-XXXX` (NEMT broker) |

**National hotline pattern:** Check Medicaid card → call "Transportation" or "NEMT" number → if none, call state Medicaid member services.

**Sources:** [Medicaid.gov — Assurance of Transportation](https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/benefits/assurance-of-transportation); [CMS NEMT Provider Booklet](https://www.cms.gov/medicare-medicaid-coordination/fraud-prevention/medicaid-integrity-education/downloads/nemt-booklet.pdf); companion state table.

### 2. Medicare Advantage supplemental rides

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Enrollees in Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans that **elect** to offer a transportation supplemental benefit. More common in Special Needs Plans (D-SNPs, C-SNPs: ~67–81%) than standard MA (~22–30% in 2026). SSBCI plans may add non-medical transport (grocery) for chronically ill members — individually targeted. |
| **Trips covered** | Plan-defined: typically medical appointments, pharmacy, dialysis, therapy, sometimes fitness centers. SSBCI may include grocery or social determinants trips. **Original Medicare (Parts A & B) does not cover routine rides.** |
| **How to request** | Call **transportation number on member ID card** or member services. Ask plan's vendor (ModivCare, SafeRide, etc.). Advance notice: **24 hours to 3+ days** depending on plan. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Benefit is **optional and shrinking**; trip caps (e.g., 24–48 one-way/year); plan-approved locations only; may require prior authorization; dual-eligibles should verify coordination with Medicaid NEMT; Uber/Lyft may be backend vendor but patient still books through plan. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Call the number on your Medicare insurance card to ask if free rides are included.` |
| **transportPhone** | Plan-specific vendor line from member card; fallback instruction: `1-800-MEDICARE` for plan lookup only — **not** a ride scheduler |

**Clinician script:** "Do you have the red-white-and-blue Medicare card only, or a separate insurance card from a company like UnitedHealthcare, Humana, or Aetna? If separate, call that card's member services."

**Sources:** [KFF — MA in 2026](https://www.kff.org/medicare/medicare-advantage-in-2026-premiums-out-of-pocket-limits-supplemental-benefits-and-prior-authorization/); [Medicare.gov — Ambulance](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/ambulance-services).

### 3. Veterans — VA travel & volunteer transport

#### 3a. DAV Volunteer Transportation Network

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Veterans enrolled in VA health care traveling to **VA medical facilities** for scheduled appointments. Volunteer availability varies — not every community covered. |
| **Trips covered** | VA medical center / VA clinic appointments. Some regions coordinate with county Veterans Affairs offices. |
| **How to request** | Contact **DAV Hospital Service Coordinator** at nearest VA facility. Use [DAV Hospital Service Coordinator Directory](https://www.dav.org/get-help-now/medical-transportation/). Schedule **as early as possible** — some sites request 2 weeks; weekday daytime only. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Volunteer-staffed — coverage gaps common; no weekend/holiday in many locations; wheelchair capacity limited; **using DAV van disqualifies same-trip Beneficiary Travel reimbursement**; not for non-VA providers unless VA-authorized community care. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Ask the VA clinic about free DAV van rides to your VA appointments.` |
| **transportPhone** | Local VA facility patient transport line; DAV HSC directory lookup; pattern: `(XXX) XXX-XXXX — VA Patient Transportation` |

#### 3b. Veterans Transportation Service (VTS) / VetRide

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Veterans with VA health care eligibility and a **VA-authorized appointment** (VA or authorized non-VA). |
| **Trips covered** | VA and VA-authorized non-VA medical appointments. |
| **How to request** | Some facilities use **VetRide Travel Portal** (online, 24 hr/day scheduling). Others: call facility VTS office. **24+ hours advance** typical. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Not available at all VA sites; first-come-first-served; short-notice availability very limited; not for emergency ambulance needs. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Call your VA hospital to schedule a free ride to your VA appointment.` |
| **transportPhone** | Facility-specific; search `site:va.gov [facility name] transportation` |

#### 3c. VA Beneficiary Travel (reimbursement — not upfront free)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Veterans meeting financial/medical criteria: typically ≥30% service-connected disability, VA pension recipients, or income below MT threshold; special mode if medically necessary and pre-approved. |
| **Trips covered** | Reimbursement for mileage, tolls, meals/lodging (if eligible), common carrier, or approved special-mode transport to **VA or VA-authorized care**. |
| **How to request** | File within **30 days** via [BTSSS portal](https://www.va.gov/health-care/file-travel-pay-reimbursement/) or VA Form 10-3542. **Patient pays upfront**, VA reimburses later. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **Not a ride service** — reimbursement only; cannot combine with free DAV/VTS for same trip; special mode requires VA pre-approval. |
| **patientBlurb** | `You may get gas money back from the VA after your trip — ask the VA travel office.` |
| **transportPhone** | `1-855-574-7296` (VA Beneficiary Travel help) or local VA travel office |

**Sources:** [VA Veterans Transportation Program](https://www.va.gov/healthbenefits/vtp/); [DAV Medical Transportation](https://www.dav.org/get-help-now/medical-transportation/); [VA Travel Reimbursement](https://www.va.gov/health-care/file-travel-pay-reimbursement/).

### 4. 211 / local mobility referral

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Anyone — 211 specialists assess local programs. Many sub-programs target low-income, seniors, disabled, or uninsured. No national income test. |
| **Trips covered** | Varies locally: medical appointments, food access, employment, shelter, veteran services, pharmacy, behavioral health. 211 maintains databases by county. |
| **How to request** | **Dial 211** (or `1-800-XXX-XXXX` where 211 unavailable). Text **898211** with zip code in many regions. Online: [211.org](https://www.211.org/). |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | 211 **refers** — does not always dispatch rides directly; wait times vary; rural coverage thinner; may redirect to Medicaid NEMT if eligible; not emergency transport. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Dial 211 to find free ride programs near you.` |
| **transportPhone** | `211` or `1-800-436-9997` (example regional); always list local 211 backup number if known |

**Sources:** [211.org](https://www.211.org/); [Nevada 211 — Transportation](https://www.nevada211.org/transportation/).

### 5. Ride United (United Way + Lyft)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Low-income individuals in **participating United Way / 211 service areas** with unmet transportation need. Eligibility vetted by 211 specialist or partner agency. |
| **Trips covered** | Medical/mental health/substance use appointments, employment, food, housing, legal, education — **essential destinations only** (not convenience). |
| **How to request** | Call **local 211** or participating United Way chapter. Some areas: direct United Way line (e.g., New Orleans `504-827-6858`). Same-day or advance depending on funding. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **Not nationwide** — county-specific; limited monthly ride caps (e.g., 4 one-way/month in some areas); requires smartphone for Lyft pass in some programs; funding runs out; separate from 211 information-only calls in some regions; patient may owe balance if ride exceeds credit. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Call 211 and ask about Ride United free Lyft rides in your area.` |
| **transportPhone** | `211` → Ride United; or local United Way transport line |

**Sources:** [United Way — Ride United](https://www.unitedway.org/ride-united); [United Way SELA — Ride United](https://www.unitedwaysela.org/ride-united/).

### 6. ADA complementary paratransit

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | People whose **functional disability** prevents independent use of fixed-route public transit (bus/rail), regardless of diagnosis. Eligibility based on functional assessment — not diagnosis alone. Temporary eligibility possible. |
| **Trips covered** | Any destination within **¾-mile corridor** of fixed routes during service hours — **not medical-only** (can use for pharmacy, therapy, grocery within service area). |
| **How to apply** | Contact **local transit agency** paratransit/Access office. Application + often in-person interview or functional evaluation. Processing: **up to 21 days** (interim rides may be allowed if delayed). |
| **How to book trips** | Phone or app with certified provider. **Advance reservation required** — typically by **5:00 PM the day before** (varies); same-day only if capacity allows (unreliable). |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **Not same-day for new applicants**; fare often required ($2–$4 typical — reduced from full transit fare, not always $0); pickup window ±20–60 min; subscription trips for recurring medical appts recommended; does not serve areas outside fixed-route corridor. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Call your local bus company to apply for disability van rides — it takes a few weeks.` |
| **transportPhone** | Local paratransit booking line; pattern: `(XXX) XXX-XXXX — [City] Paratransit / Access-A-Ride` |

**Sources:** [National RTAP — ADA Paratransit](https://www.nationalrtap.org/Toolkits/ADA-Toolkit/Service-Type-Requirements/ADA-Complementary-Paratransit-Requirements); [FTA ADA Circular 4710.1](https://www.transit.dot.gov/regulations-and-guidance/civil-rights-ada/ada-guidance).

### 7. Public transit — reduced fare & free-ride programs

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | **Seniors** (often 65+, sometimes 60+), **people with disabilities** (certification required), **Medicare cardholders**, sometimes **Medicaid/SSI** recipients (state-specific). Illinois Benefit Access: **free** fixed-route for qualifying seniors/disabled. Most programs: **reduced fare**, not free. |
| **Trips covered** | Any trip on fixed-route bus/rail — medical, pharmacy, work, personal. Medicaid card alone rarely qualifies; Medicare card often accepted as proof. |
| **How to apply** | Local transit agency website or customer service center. Application + ID + disability form (provider-signed in many systems). Processing: days to weeks. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **Not door-to-door**; patient must reach stop; may not be feasible during mania, psychosis, or mobility crisis; fare still required unless in free-ride program; Medicaid NEMT may require using transit first if stop within ¼–¾ mile. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Ask your local bus company about cheaper fares for seniors or disability.` |
| **transportPhone** | Local transit customer service; pattern: `(XXX) XXX-XXXX — [Agency] Reduced Fare` |

**Examples:** NYC MTA Reduced Fare (`511` or MTA Reduced Fare office); Chicago RTA (`312-913-3110`); NJ Transit Reduced Fare Program.

**Sources:** [MTA Reduced Fare](https://www.mta.info/fares-tolls/subway-bus/reduced-fare); [CTA/RTA Reduced Fare](https://www.transitchicago.com/reduced-fare-programs/).

### 8. Hospital / clinic shuttle & charity rides

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Established patients of health systems offering shuttle service or charity transport — often uninsured, underinsured, or those failing NEMT eligibility. **Facility-specific.** |
| **Trips covered** | Round-trip to **that health system's clinics/hospitals**; sometimes pharmacy near campus. |
| **How to request** | Ask **social work, case management, or clinic front desk** at discharge. Some systems (e.g., L.A. County DHS) offer Uber/Lyft vouchers for non-NEMT-eligible patients — **clinic must order**. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Only to system facilities; limited hours; requires advance booking through staff; not all hospitals offer; may be one-time (discharge only) vs. ongoing. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Ask your clinic social worker if the hospital offers free shuttle rides.` |
| **transportPhone** | Facility patient transport / social work line; pattern: `(XXX) XXX-XXXX — [Hospital] Patient Transport` |

**Example:** L.A. County DHS partners with Uber/Lyft for clinic patients not eligible for Medi-Cal NEMT — contact must go through clinic staff ([LAC DHS Transportation](https://dhs.lacounty.gov/our-services/resources/transportation/)).

### 9. Nonprofit & volunteer ride programs

#### 9a. American Cancer Society — Road To Recovery

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | People with **cancer diagnosis** traveling to cancer-related treatment appointments. |
| **Trips covered** | Cancer treatment appointments only. |
| **How to request** | Call **`1-800-227-2345`** at least **4 business days** ahead. Volunteer driver matched by zip code. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Volunteer availability — **many requests unmet** post-COVID (~40–60% fulfillment in some counties); not for non-cancer care; not psychiatric-specific unless cancer-related oncology psych. |
| **patientBlurb** | `If you have cancer, call 1-800-227-2345 for free ride help.` |
| **transportPhone** | `1-800-227-2345` |

#### 9b. Area Agency on Aging (AAA) volunteer drivers

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Typically adults **60+** and sometimes younger adults with disabilities. County-specific. |
| **Trips covered** | Medical appointments primary; some programs add grocery, pharmacy. |
| **How to request** | Call **Eldercare Locator `1-800-677-1116`** or local Council on Aging / AAA. Advance notice: **1–2 weeks** common. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Volunteer-dependent — rides not guaranteed; geographic limits; may not accommodate wheelchairs; weekday daytime only. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Call 1-800-677-1116 to find free senior ride programs near you.` |
| **transportPhone** | `1-800-677-1116` (Eldercare Locator) → local AAA program |

#### 9c. Faith-based ride ministries & local MedRide programs

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Varies — often congregation members first, then community referrals. No national eligibility standard. |
| **Trips covered** | Usually medical appointments; some expand to grocery/pharmacy for homebound seniors. |
| **How to request** | 211 referral; hospital chaplain; local church/synagogue/mosque office. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | Informal scheduling; insurance/background check varies; not reliable for court-ordered treatment or strict appointment times; capacity very limited. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Dial 211 to ask about church or volunteer ride programs near you.` |
| **transportPhone** | `211` → local referral; no national number |

**Sources:** [ACS Road To Recovery](https://www.cancer.org/involved/volunteer/road-to-recovery.html); [USAging — Transportation](https://www.usaging.org/transportation); [Mass.gov — Volunteer Driver Programs](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/volunteer-driver-programs).

### 11. Crisis & mobile response teams (988 / MCT)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | Anyone experiencing a **behavioral health or substance use crisis** — suicidal ideation, psychosis, severe agitation, overdose concern. |
| **Trips covered** | Transport to **crisis stabilization, ED, or inpatient** when team determines necessary as part of crisis response — **warm handoff only**. Washington MCR may also transport to pharmacy/food bank **during active crisis resolution** (state-specific). |
| **How to request** | **Call or text 988**; some states: county crisis line. Mobile team dispatched when phone support insufficient. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **NOT routine outpatient transport**; not for scheduled therapy follow-up next week; availability 24/7 in ~20 states for MCT; rural response times vary (2–24 hr tiers); may involve law enforcement/EMS if safety requires; Colorado explicitly states MCR is **not** substitute for ongoing scheduled treatment transport. |
| **patientBlurb** | `If you are in crisis now, call or text 988 — they can send help to you.` |
| **transportPhone** | `988` (crisis); `911` (immediate danger) |

**Sources:** [SAMHSA — 988 FAQs](https://www.samhsa.gov/mental-health/988/faqs); [CMS SHO 21-008 — Mobile Crisis](https://www.medicaid.gov/federal-policy-guidance/downloads/sho21008.pdf); [WA MCR Program Guide](https://www.hca.wa.gov/assets/program/mobile-crisis-response-program-guide.pdf).

### 12. Rideshare charity credits (Lyft / Uber healthcare)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Who qualifies** | **No direct public program.** Access only through: Medicaid/MA NEMT broker (vendor may dispatch Lyft/Uber), hospital/clinic voucher program, Ride United, or Uber Health / Lyft Healthcare **B2B partnerships**. |
| **Trips covered** | Medical appointments when ordered by qualifying organization. |
| **How to request** | Patient **cannot** call Uber/Lyft for free medical ride directly. Must go through insurance broker, 211/Ride United, or clinic social work. |
| **Limitations / NOT free caveats** | **Lyft/Uber discontinued most direct patient charity programs**; Uber Health serves 3,000+ healthcare orgs but billing is to the org/plan; Mississippi Dept of Health pilot (Uber Health) is state-specific; patient may face surge pricing if voucher doesn't cover full fare. |
| **patientBlurb** | `Free Uber or Lyft rides must be set up by your clinic or insurance — ask them.` |
| **transportPhone** | N/A direct; route through NEMT broker, 211, or clinic social work |

**Sources:** [United Way — Ride United / Lyft partnership](https://www.unitedway.org/news/more-programs-offering-low-cost-reliable-transportation-for-health-care-visits); [KFF Health News — Lyft/Uber medical transport](https://kffhealthnews.org/medicaid/lyft-uber-medical-georgia-atlanta-transport-rural-hospitals/).

**Rules:**
- Prefer **one primary number** per resource entry; put backup instructions in `access` field.

### Discharge packet block (clinician script)

> **Getting to appointments:** Free rides are **not automatic**. If you have **Medicaid**, call the transportation number on your card **at least 2–3 business days** before each visit. If you have **Medicare insurance through a private company**, call the number on your card to ask if rides are included. **Veterans:** ask your VA clinic about free van rides. **No insurance or still stuck?** Dial **211** to find local programs. If you **cannot use regular buses** because of disability, apply for **paratransit** through your local transit agency (this takes a few weeks). **In a crisis now?** Call or text **988** — not for regular appointment rides.

## Top 5 most reliable options nationally

| Rank | Option | Why reliable | Main limitation |
|------|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Medicaid NEMT** | Federally required benefit; established broker infrastructure in 46+ states | Not automatic; waiver states; advance notice; medical only |
| 2 | **Medicare Advantage transport benefit** | Contractual trip allowance when included; professional dispatch | Only ~1 in 4 individual MA plans; shrinking; plan-specific |
| 3 | **211 / Ride United** | National network; fills uninsured and gap cases; human referral | Not available everywhere; funding caps; not same-day guaranteed |
| 4 | **VA DAV / VTS** | Free volunteer/professional transport to VA care | Veterans only; VA destinations; volunteer gaps |
| 5 | **ADA paratransit** | Legal entitlement once certified; recurring medical trips workable | Weeks to certify; not free in all systems; ±long pickup windows |

**Honorable mentions:** AAA volunteer drivers (strong locally, weak nationally), hospital charity Uber/Lyft ( excellent when available, not systematic), reduced-fare transit (low cost, not free).
