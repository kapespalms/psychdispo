# Medicaid & Medicare Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT)
## Research reference for PsychDispo discharge handouts

**Prepared:** June 2026  
**Audience:** Clinicians building psychiatric/social discharge resource packets  
**Scope:** 50 states + DC — coverage rules, limitations, broker contacts, patient wording

---

## Executive summary (for clinicians)

**Rides are not automatically available** for patients with Medicare or Medicaid. Transportation is one of the most variable benefits in U.S. health coverage, and discharge planners should never tell a patient that a ride is guaranteed.

**Medicare:** Original Medicare (Parts A & B) does **not** cover routine rides to outpatient appointments, pharmacy, therapy, or follow-up visits. Part B covers **ambulance** only when other transport would endanger the patient's health, or in limited non-emergency ambulance cases with a physician order and (for repetitive trips) prior authorization. Medicare Advantage (Part C) *may* add a supplemental transportation benefit, but it is **optional and plan-specific** — roughly **22–30%** of individual MA plans offered it in 2026, down from 36% in 2024. Dual-eligible patients may have Medicaid NEMT instead of (or in addition to) MA benefits.

**Medicaid:** Federal law requires states to assure **necessary transportation** to Medicaid-covered services (42 CFR §431.53; codified in the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021). EPSDT-eligible children and youth (generally under 21) have a **stronger** transportation mandate. However, **how** NEMT is delivered varies sharply by state: broker vs. county office vs. health plan; advance scheduling (often 2–5 business days); medical necessity and prior authorization; public-transit-first rules; mileage caps; and rural provider shortages. Several states received **Section 1115 waiver** authority to **exclude NEMT** for certain adult expansion populations (notably Georgia Pathways, Indiana HIP, Iowa Wellness Plan, Kentucky TEAMKY). Even where NEMT exists on paper, **same-day rides, pharmacy-only trips, and social (non-medical) destinations** are often excluded or hard to obtain.

**Clinical takeaway:** Always give patients the **specific phone number** for their state's NEMT broker or county program, tell them to **call several business days before** the appointment, and have a **backup plan** (family, taxi voucher program, 211, transit pass). For Medicare-only patients, check the **Medicare Advantage Evidence of Coverage** or call **1-800-MEDICARE** — do not assume coverage.

## 1. Medicare transportation

### 1.1 Original Medicare (Fee-for-Service)

| Service | Covered? | Key rules |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Routine NEMT (car/van to clinic, therapy, pharmacy) | **No** | Not a Medicare benefit |
| Emergency ground/air ambulance | **Yes**, if medically necessary | Must be to nearest appropriate facility; other transport would endanger health |
| Non-emergency ambulance | **Limited** | Physician order required; bed-confined or condition contraindicates other transport; repetitive scheduled trips need prior authorization (3+ round trips/10 days or weekly × 3 weeks) |
| Wheelchair van / medical car | **No** (routine) | Unless billed as medically necessary ambulance meeting CMS criteria |

**Sources:** [Medicare.gov — Ambulance services](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/ambulance-services); [CMS MLN — Ambulance Services](https://www.cms.gov/training-education/medicare-learning-networkr-mln/compliance/medicare-provider-compliance-tips/ambulance-services); Medicare Publication 11021 (June 2023).

### 1.2 Medicare Advantage (Part C)

MA plans must cover everything Original Medicare covers (including ambulance rules above). **Supplemental transportation** (rides to medical appointments in a car/van) is **not required** and varies by plan, county, and year.

| Population | Approx. plans with transportation benefit | Notes |
|------------|-------------------------------------------|-------|
| Individual MA plans (non-SNP) | **~22–30%** in 2026 (down from 36% in 2024) | Trip caps common (e.g., 24–48 one-way trips/year) |
| Special Needs Plans (SNPs) | **~67–81%** in 2025–2026 | D-SNPs more likely; still plan-specific |
| Dual-eligible (D-SNP) | Often yes, but verify | May coordinate with Medicaid NEMT — avoid duplicate assumptions |

Benefit types (not mutually exclusive):
- **Primarily health-related supplemental benefits** — medical appointment transport; must be uniform within similarly situated enrollees.
- **SSBCI (Special Supplemental Benefits for the Chronically Ill)** — can include non-medical transport (e.g., grocery) for qualifying chronically ill members; individually targeted.
- **VBID model plans** — targeted supplemental benefits by condition/income in participating plans.

**What to tell MA patients:** Call the **member services number on the insurance card** and ask: (1) Is transportation a benefit this plan year? (2) How many one-way trips? (3) Medical destinations only? (4) How far in advance to book? (5) Does psychiatric/behavioral health count?

**Sources:** [KFF — MA 2025 Spotlight](https://www.kff.org/medicare/medicare-advantage-2025-spotlight-a-first-look-at-plan-premiums-and-benefits/) (Oct 2024); [KFF — MA in 2026](https://www.kff.org/medicare/medicare-advantage-in-2026-premiums-out-of-pocket-limits-supplemental-benefits-and-prior-authorization/) (Oct 2025); [Commonwealth Fund — MA Supplemental Benefits](https://www.commonwealthfund.org/publications/issue-briefs/2021/feb/medicare-advantage-plans-supplemental-benefits); [ATI Advisory — SSBCI Primer](https://atiadvisory.com/resources/newly-expanded-non-medical-supplemental-benefits-in-medicare-advantage-a-primer/).

### 1.3 Medicare clear answer

> **Medicare does NOT guarantee a ride.** Original Medicare covers ambulance only under strict medical-necessity rules. Medicare Advantage may add rides, but most individual plans do not include them, benefits are shrinking, and every plan differs.

## 2. Medicaid NEMT — national rules

### 2.1 Federal requirement

- **42 CFR §431.53:** States must assure necessary transportation for Medicaid beneficiaries to and from covered services.
- **42 CFR §441.62 (EPSDT):** States must offer necessary assistance with transportation for EPSDT-eligible individuals (generally under 21), including escorts when needed.
- **Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021 (§209):** Codified transportation assurance; limits FFP unless state plans ensure efficient, economical, quality NEMT payment.
- **SMD 23-006 (June 2023):** [Medicaid Transportation Coverage Guide](https://www.medicaid.gov/federal-policy-guidance/downloads/smd23006.pdf) — consolidated federal policy.

### 2.2 Common operational requirements (all states — details vary)

| Requirement | Typical pattern |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Medical necessity** | Trip must be to/from a **Medicaid-covered** service; broker or state verifies appointment |
| **No other means** | Patient lacks car, driver, or usable public transit (many states require transit if within ¼–¾ mile of stop) |
| **Advance scheduling** | **2–5 business days** common; same-day/urgent often requires provider verification |
| **Prior authorization** | Required for specialized modes (wheelchair van, long distance); some states require physician certification (e.g., CA PCS form) |
| **Destination limits** | Medical appointments, dialysis, pharmacy (varies); **not** social visits, work, or grocery (unless separate SSBCI/waiver program) |
| **Delivery model** | **State broker** (ModivCare, MTM, etc.), **MCO-carved-in** broker, **county/region** office, or **direct FFS** reimbursement |

### 2.3 Section 1115 NEMT waivers — limited adult populations

Some states waived the NEMT assurance for **ACA Medicaid expansion adults** (not for EPSDT youth, pregnant members, or often "medically frail" categories):

| State | Program | NEMT status for affected adults | EPSDT / exceptions |
|-------|---------|--------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Georgia** | Pathways to Coverage (1115) | **No NEMT** for Pathways adults | Ages 19–20 EPSDT includes NEMT |
| **Indiana** | HIP 2.0 (1115) | **No state NEMT** for most HIP members | Pregnant, medically frail, TMA, parents/caretakers, ages 19–20; one MCE offers limited voluntary rides |
| **Iowa** | Iowa Health & Wellness Plan (1115) | **No NEMT** for IHAWP expansion adults | IA Health Link MCO members have NEMT via Access2Care; FFS via MTM; **CMS: IHAWP NEMT waiver ends Dec 31, 2026** |
| **Kentucky** | TEAMKY / KY HEALTH (1115) | **No NEMT** for most expansion adults | Medically frail, pregnant, EPSDT 19–20, former foster care; **methadone NEMT expanded to all Medicaid Sep 2023** |
| **Utah** | 1115 demonstration | **NEMT not assured** for parent/caretaker relatives unless EPSDT-eligible | Other Medicaid groups have statewide ModivCare NEMT with household-vehicle rules |

**Sources:** [Medicaid.gov — Assurance of Transportation](https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/benefits/assurance-of-transportation); [NEMT Report to Congress 2018–2020 (RTC)](https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/benefits/downloads/nemt-rtc.pdf); [KFF — NEMT & Expansion Waivers](https://www.kff.org/medicaid/medicaid-non-emergency-medical-transportation-overview-and-key-issues-in-medicaid-expansion-waivers/); [Georgia Pathways waiver extension request (Jan 2025)](https://medicaid.georgia.gov/document/document/georgia-section-1115-demonstration-waiver-extension-requestv01172025-fv0/download); [SHVS — Iowa waiver Dec 2025](https://shvs.org/states-of-innovation-resources/states-of-innovation-december-2025/); [KY DMS Provider Letter — methadone NEMT Sep 2023](https://assets.humana.com/is/content/humana/NMET_Services_for_Methadone_Treatment_Letterpdf).

### 2.4 Medicaid clear answer

> **Medicaid NEMT is not "always available."** It is federally required for most beneficiaries, but **eligibility subgroup, health plan, geography, advance notice, medical necessity, and broker capacity** all limit access. Five states restrict NEMT for certain expansion adults. Rural areas frequently report broker/provider shortages.

## 4. PsychDispo guidance

### 4.1 `patientBlurb` — recommended wording

**Do NOT use:**
- "Your ride is covered."
- "Medicaid/Medicare will take you to appointments."
- "Call for a free ride anytime."

**DO use (examples ≤80 chars):**

| Scenario | `patientBlurb` |
|----------|----------------|
| Medicaid NEMT entry | `Call 2–3 days before your visit to ask about a medical ride.` |
| Medicaid (limited state) | `Call your plan to ask if a ride is covered — not everyone qualifies.` |
| Medicare only | `Call your Medicare plan to ask if rides are a benefit this year.` |
| Dual-eligible | `Call the Medicaid ride line first; your plan may also help.` |
| General transport panel | `Call ahead — rides are not same-day and must be set up early.` |

Spanish example: `Llame 2–3 días antes para preguntar por transporte médico.`

### 4.2 `transportPhone` fields

| Field | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **When to include** | Separate NEMT/broker resource entries for Medicaid; not duplicated on every clinical site unless that site runs its own ride line |
| **Primary number** | State broker or "Medicaid Transportation" line from table above |
| **Secondary** | MCO member services if state uses carved-in MCO brokers (NH, FL, KS, etc.) — note in `access` field: "Call number on Medicaid ID card if different" |
| **Medicare** | Do **not** put 1-800-MEDICARE as `transportPhone` unless no other option; prefer "Medicare Advantage member services" instruction in `access` |
| **Backup resources** | 211, local paratransit, cancer society Road To Recovery — separate entries, not NEMT |

### 4.3 Discharge packet language (clinician script)

Include this block (or similar) on transport handouts:

> **Medical rides are not automatic.** If you have **Medicaid**, call your state's medical ride line **at least 2–3 business days** before your appointment. Trips are usually for **medical visits only** and may require approval. If you have **Medicare only**, call the number on your **insurance card** to ask if your plan includes rides — most Medicare plans do **not**. If you have **both**, try Medicaid first. Always have a backup way to get there.

### 4.4 Data entry checklist

- [ ] NEMT resource `cat` appropriate (e.g., social/transport panel F)
- [ ] `transportPhone` = broker line, verified on state Medicaid site
- [ ] `access` notes advance scheduling and medical-only limitation
- [ ] `patientBlurb` does not promise coverage
- [ ] Flag `Limited` states (GA, IN, IA, KY) with expansion-adult caveat in `svc` or `access`
- [ ] Dual-eligible: ensure both Medicaid NEMT and MA plan instructions not contradictory
